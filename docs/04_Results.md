# Results and Discussion

# 1. Introduction

The primary objective of this study was to investigate the influence of injector geometry on the aerodynamic and combustion characteristics of a hydrogen-fueled scramjet combustor. Three different injector configurations—Wedge Strut, Lobed Strut, and Diamond Strut—were numerically analyzed under identical operating conditions using Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD).

The comparison was performed by examining important flow parameters including pressure distribution, velocity field, density variation, temperature distribution, Mach number, turbulent mixing, and combustion characteristics. Since all numerical conditions remained identical throughout the investigation, the observed variations can be attributed directly to differences in injector geometry.

Rather than evaluating only a single performance parameter, the injectors were assessed based on their overall engineering performance. In practical scramjet design, maximizing combustion efficiency alone is not sufficient. An effective injector must also preserve total pressure, minimize aerodynamic losses, and generate stable combustion while maintaining efficient supersonic flow.

---

# 2. Pressure Distribution

Pressure contours provide valuable insight into the interaction between the incoming supersonic airflow and the injector geometry.

As the airflow encounters the injector leading edge, oblique shock waves are generated. Across these shocks, the static pressure increases while the Mach number decreases. The magnitude and location of these pressure changes depend strongly on injector geometry.

### Wedge Strut

The wedge strut generated relatively stable oblique shock waves with moderate pressure rise. The pressure distribution remained smooth throughout the combustor, indicating controlled compression and reduced aerodynamic losses.

The moderate pressure increase also contributed to stable downstream combustion without excessive total pressure loss.

### Lobed Strut

The lobed injector produced more complex pressure distributions due to its multiple lobes.

Each lobe generated localized shock structures which interacted with one another, producing larger pressure gradients throughout the combustor.

Although these stronger shock interactions improved turbulent mixing, they also increased irreversible pressure losses.

### Diamond Strut

The diamond injector produced comparatively weaker pressure disturbances because of its streamlined geometry.

The pressure contours remained relatively uniform, indicating lower aerodynamic resistance. However, the weaker compression also reduced the effectiveness of fuel-air mixing.

Overall, the wedge injector demonstrated the most balanced pressure characteristics.

---

# 3. Velocity Distribution

Velocity contours describe how the incoming supersonic airflow is modified after interacting with the injector.

As expected, the flow decelerated after passing through the oblique shock waves generated by each injector.

### Wedge Strut

The wedge injector produced gradual velocity reduction while maintaining stable downstream flow.

Large regions of flow separation were not observed, allowing efficient transport of hydrogen throughout the combustor.

### Lobed Strut

The lobed injector produced stronger velocity gradients due to increased vortex generation.

The velocity field became highly non-uniform as the flow interacted with multiple recirculation zones.

Although these vortices enhanced hydrogen-air mixing, they also increased viscous dissipation.

### Diamond Strut

The diamond injector preserved higher flow velocity because it introduced smaller disturbances into the incoming airflow.

However, the weaker interaction between fuel and air reduced mixing efficiency.

---

# 4. Density Distribution

Density variations are characteristic of compressible supersonic flow.

Across shock waves, density increases significantly because the airflow undergoes rapid compression.

The density contours observed during the simulations clearly identified the locations of major shock structures.

The wedge injector produced well-organized density gradients corresponding to stable oblique shocks.

The lobed injector generated highly irregular density fields because multiple shock interactions occurred simultaneously.

The diamond injector exhibited comparatively smoother density distributions with weaker compressibility effects.

---

# 5. Temperature Distribution

Temperature contours illustrate the energy released during hydrogen combustion.

Regions of elevated temperature correspond to areas where combustion is more intense.

The wedge injector produced a relatively uniform temperature field, indicating efficient combustion over a larger region of the combustor.

The lobed injector generated localized regions of very high temperature due to enhanced turbulent mixing and rapid combustion.

Although higher temperatures generally indicate stronger combustion, they may also increase thermal loading on combustor walls.

The diamond injector produced comparatively lower temperatures because weaker mixing reduced combustion intensity.

---

# 6. Mach Number Distribution

The Mach number contours describe the compressible flow characteristics inside the combustor.

Before reaching the injector, the incoming airflow remained supersonic.

Upon encountering the injector, oblique shock waves reduced the local Mach number.

The wedge injector maintained a relatively uniform supersonic flow while producing sufficient compression for combustion.

The lobed injector caused larger reductions in local Mach number because of stronger shock-wave interactions.

The diamond injector preserved higher Mach numbers throughout much of the combustor due to weaker compression.

Maintaining supersonic combustion while avoiding excessive deceleration is one of the key design challenges addressed by this study.

---

# 7. Shock-Wave Structures

Shock-wave formation is one of the defining characteristics of scramjet flow.

The injector geometry determines:

- Shock angle
- Shock strength
- Shock interaction
- Downstream pressure recovery

The wedge injector generated clean oblique shock waves with relatively predictable behavior.

The lobed injector produced multiple interacting shock systems that increased turbulence but also raised total pressure losses.

The diamond injector generated weaker shock structures with reduced mixing capability.

These observations demonstrate that injector geometry has a direct influence on compressible flow behavior.

---

# 8. Turbulent Mixing

Efficient hydrogen-air mixing is essential because combustion must occur within a residence time of only a few milliseconds.

The wedge injector produced moderate turbulence, providing an effective balance between mixing and pressure recovery.

The lobed injector generated the strongest vortices, significantly increasing turbulent mixing and combustion efficiency.

However, the increased turbulence also increased aerodynamic losses.

The diamond injector generated comparatively weak vortices, resulting in lower mixing efficiency.

---

# 9. Combustion Performance

Combustion performance depends primarily on the effectiveness of hydrogen-air mixing.

The simulations indicate that injector geometry strongly influences flame development through its effect on turbulence generation.

Among the three injector configurations, the lobed injector demonstrated the strongest mixing capability.

The wedge injector achieved nearly comparable combustion performance while maintaining superior pressure recovery.

The diamond injector produced lower combustion efficiency because weaker vortices delayed mixing.

---

# 10. Pressure Recovery

Pressure recovery is an important propulsion performance parameter because excessive pressure losses reduce engine thrust.

The wedge injector demonstrated the best pressure recovery among the three geometries.

The lobed injector experienced the greatest pressure losses because of increased turbulence and stronger shock interactions.

The diamond injector preserved pressure reasonably well but sacrificed combustion efficiency.

These observations highlight the engineering trade-off between efficient combustion and pressure recovery.

# 12. Engineering Significance

The results obtained in this investigation demonstrate that injector geometry has a significant influence on the aerodynamic and combustion performance of a scramjet combustor.

A successful injector design must satisfy several competing objectives simultaneously:

- Enhance hydrogen-air mixing
- Maintain stable combustion
- Preserve total pressure
- Minimize aerodynamic losses
- Generate controlled shock-wave structures

The wedge injector provided the best compromise among these competing requirements.

---

# 13. Conclusion

The CFD analysis confirms that injector geometry plays a decisive role in determining scramjet combustor performance.

Although the lobed injector generated superior turbulent mixing, the associated pressure losses reduced its overall effectiveness.

The diamond injector minimized aerodynamic losses but produced comparatively weaker combustion.

Among the three configurations investigated, the wedge injector achieved the most balanced overall performance by combining efficient fuel-air mixing with good pressure recovery and stable compressible flow behavior.

The findings of this research contribute to the understanding of injector design for future hypersonic propulsion systems and demonstrate the capability of Computational Fluid Dynamics as a powerful engineering tool for analyzing complex reacting flows.
